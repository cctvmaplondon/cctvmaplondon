CCTV Map London
About the project
Surveillance in London is ubiquitous. Everyone is aware of CCTV but not everyone understands how it really works and how much information can be gleaned from this form of monitoring.
Transport for London (TfL) has thousands of cameras in London that operate daily, capturing and storing personal data. TfL shares certain data and camera feeds with third parties like police and local authorities. In some cases, the street cameras can be fully controlled by a third party.

TfL Subject Access Request
You can request personal data the TfL has on you under the Data Protection Act 1998. However, you must provide enough satisfactory information and pay an administrative fee.

WhatDoTheyKnow
WhatDoTheyKnow is a site that allows you to make a Freedom of Information request for information to any UK public authority. By law the authority must respond in some way.
